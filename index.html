About Me

My Journey

My story is unique. I grew up in Bnei Brak, the eldest of eight children in a Haredi (ultra-Orthodox) family. My late father was a full-time Talmud scholar, while my mother works as a teacher for children with special needs. From a young age, I questioned the Haredi education I received. At 15, I found the courage to explore beyond my community and moved in with non-orthodox relatives. Leaving the Haredi community meant I had no basic knowledge in mathematics or English. I didn't even know the ABCs, and my arithmetic knowledge was limited to multiplication tables. Unfamiliar with most core subjects, I was determined to bridge the gap and pursued the Bagrut (Israeli SATs). I enrolled in an external school and later completed most of the Bagrut qualifications while also studying fitness training at the Wingate Institute.

At 18, I lived with relatives who took me in. Like many youths in their area, I attended a pre-military academy called Emek HaMaayanot near Beit She'an. There, we immersed ourselves in contemporary spiritual philosophy and Zionism, preparing physically and mentally for military service. Despite initially being refused by the IDF due to a low medical profile, I volunteered for national service after several unsuccessful appeals. In the first year, I served at Jordan River Village, and in the second, with Magen David Adom (MDA).

After my service, I completed my matriculation exams and worked as a paramedic. When the COVID-19 pandemic struck, I found myself with an abundance of free time. Eager to learn, I enrolled in an online programming course. My interest in programming and computing grew rapidly, leading me to pursue a degree in Computer Science at Reichman University.

During my degree I joined KoaliTime, where we help parents monitor and manage their children's usage of mobile apps. Our goal is to ensure quality time and promote the use of skill-building apps.
