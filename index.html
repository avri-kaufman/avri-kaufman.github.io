About Me

I am 29 years old and in my final year of computer science studies at 'Reichman University', where I am on the 'Keren Or' scholarship track. I currently work as a Software Developer at 'KoaliTime' startup. I live in Tel Aviv with my newlywed wife, Rotem, and our adorable cat, Ray.

My story is unique. I grew up in Bnei Brak in a family of eight, with me being the eldest child. My late father was a full-time Talmud scholar (Avrech), while my mother works as a teacher in a school for children with special needs. From a young age, I had questions and doubts about the Haredi (ultra-Orthodox) education I received. At 15, I gathered the courage to explore further and left home to live with non-orthodox relatives for several years. Departing the Haredi community, I had no basic knowledge in mathematics or English. I didn't even know the ABCs, and my arithmetic knowledge was limited to multiplication tables. I was unfamiliar with most core subjects. Determined to bridge the gap, I pursued the Bagrut (Israeli SATs). I enrolled in an external school and later completed most of the Bagrut qualifications. Concurrently, I studied at the Wingate Institute for a fitness training course.

At 18, I lived with relatives who took me in. Like many youths in their area, I attended a pre-military academy called 'Emek HaMaayanot' near Beit She'an. There, we immersed ourselves in contemporary spiritual philosophy and Zionism, and they prepared us physically and mentally for military service. During my time at the academy, the IDF initially refused to draft me due to a low medical profile. After attempting to appeal the decision over several months and facing rejection from three special committees, I opted to volunteer for national service. In the first year, I served at 'Jordan River Village', and in the second, with Magen David Adom (MDA).

After my service, I completed my matriculation exams and worked as a paramedic. When the COVID-19 pandemic struck, I, like many, found myself with an abundance of free time. Eager to learn, I enrolled in an online programming course. My interest piqued rapidly, and I delved deep into the realms of programming and computing. Through a friend, I learned about the Keren Or scholarship program for computer studies at Reichman University. Seeing it as an excellent opportunity, I applied. After successfully navigating the screening process, which included mathematical and personality tests, I eagerly embarked on my academic journey, feeling that all my efforts in the matriculation exams had paid off.

Now, in my final year, I work at KoaliTime - which will help parents monitor their children's usage of mobile apps, to ensure quality time and usage of skill-building apps while using their devices. I relish the challenges and critical thinking demanded by my courses. I believe I've found my calling, a domain that consistently pushes and nurtures my growth. Many facets of the high-tech world intrigue me, and I aspire to work in a role that either adds value to the Health-Tech sector or enhances the quality of life for many.
